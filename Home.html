Understanding Global Hunger

Hunger is not just about missing a meal â€” it's a severe lack of access to food that affects over 735 million people worldwide. It leads to malnutrition, weak immune systems, and stunted growth in children.

The causes of hunger are deeply rooted in poverty, conflict, climate change, and economic instability. Many communities are trapped in cycles of food insecurity with limited access to clean water, education, or jobs.

While enough food is produced globally, poor distribution, food waste, and inequality mean that millions still suffer every day. Solving hunger requires coordinated global and local action.
